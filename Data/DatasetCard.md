## Dataset Summary 

The Silvery Blue Project is a collaboration between the California Academy of Sciences and the San Francisco Presidio Trust to restore the sand dunes to thier natural habitat, using the Silvery blue butterfly population stability as an indicator of success. This data includes survyes prior to release, release data, and post-release surveys. 

## Languages 

English 

## Data Instances 

A typical data point comprises of one Silvery blue butterfly sighting in the the field.

An example from the dataset looks as follows:

```json
{
  'date': '4/5/26',
  'butterfly id': '1',
  'sex': 'm',
  'time (24 hh)': '12:10',
  'distance (m)': '2',
  'height (m)': '0.5',
}
```

## Data Fields  

data:  the date of data collection written in month/day/year 

butterfly id: unique number given to each butterfly as an identifier

sex: the sex of the butterfly represented as m for male and f for female

time (24hh): the time of the data collection for that individual 

distance (m): the distance between the researcher and the butterfly in observation (if the butterfly is in flight provide a rough mean estimate)

height (m): the height above the ground the butterfly is observed (perched or flying)


## Discussion of Biases 

Data collected on release days are collected by researchers and volunteers which can result in varying responses and reliability of the data. This project worked towards maintaining accessibility to the community which can result in less reliable data as volunteers do not have the same training as researchers. 

## Other Known Limitations 

The data collected for this research is limited based on availability of people to survey. We have great success for releases of butterflies but it is equally important to track them in the days that follow a release. We survey daily following release until they are not sighted for three consecutive days.
